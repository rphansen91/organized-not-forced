## 1. PACKAGING: Book Format & Production

### Recommended Book Structure

Based on comparable books analysis:

| Format | Word Count | Chapters | Target |
|--------|------------|----------|--------|
| **Primary (recommended)** | 50,000-70,000 | 12-15 | Self-help/memoir hybrid |
| Comparable: Atomic Habits | 52,000 | 20 | Principle-based |
| Comparable: Can't Hurt Me | 91,000 | 11 | Transformation memoir |

**Your current chapters (~800 words each) need expansion.** The industry standard for your genre is 3,000-5,000 words per chapter. Your "Struggle" sections specifically need depth—readers need to feel the wall before the breakthrough lands.

### Multi-Format Strategy (Essential in 2026)

Authors who publish in all three formats consistently outperform single-format authors:

| Format | Priority | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **eBook** | Launch first | $4.99-$9.99 price point for 70% royalties |
| **Paperback** | Same-day launch | 6x9 trim size standard for this genre |
| **Audiobook** | Within 3-6 months | Fastest-growing format—essential for fitness/self-help |
| Hardcover | Optional | Good for gifts/credibility |

### Audiobook Considerations (Critical for Fitness Books)

Self-improvement audiobooks are one of the fastest-growing sectors. Listeners consume content while commuting, exercising, and doing daily tasks—perfect alignment with your fitness audience.

**Production Options 2026:**

| Option | Cost | Timeline | Best For |
|--------|------|----------|----------|
| Human narrator | $2,000-$10,000 | 3-8 weeks | Premium quality, emotional nuance |
| AI narration | $50-$500 | Hours to days | Budget-friendly, faster production |
| Author-narrated | Equipment + time | Variable | Personal connection, authenticity |

**AI Narration Update:** In 2026, AI voices have reached emotional nuance suitable for nonfiction. Self-help is among the most popular audiobook categories. However, ACX (Amazon) still restricts AI voices for non-exclusive titles.

**Recommended approach:** Author-narrated or professional narrator for authenticity—your book's felt experience would benefit from a personal voice.

## 2. DISTRIBUTION STRATEGY

### The Hybrid Workflow (2026 Best Practice)

Don't pick just one platform. The recommended approach:

1. **Amazon KDP** — For Amazon sales (ebook + print)
2. **IngramSpark** — For bookstore/library accessibility
3. **Draft2Digital** — For all other ebook storefronts (Apple, Kobo, B&N)
4. **Wide audiobook distribution** — Author's Republic or Voices by INaudio

### Why Go Wide vs. Amazon Exclusive?

| Exclusive (KDP Select) | Wide Distribution |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Access to Kindle Unlimited | Your book is "everywhere" |
| 70% royalty on KU reads | Legitimacy for speaking, podcasts |
| Limited to Amazon only | Long-term audience building |
| | Better for nonfiction/thought leadership |

**For your book:** Wide distribution is better. Non-fiction authors benefit from the "branding exercise" of appearing everywhere. Podcast hosts, event bookers, and speaking opportunities look for authors with broad distribution.

### ISBN Strategy

- Get your own ISBNs from Bowker ($125 for 10) for professional legitimacy
- Amazon's free ISBN limits distribution options
- One ISBN per format (ebook, paperback, hardcover, audiobook)

### Key Distribution Platforms

**eBooks:**
- Amazon KDP (dominant - 70% of readers)
- Apple Books
- Kobo (strong in Canada, UK, Europe)
- Barnes & Noble Press
- Google Play Books

**Print:**
- Amazon KDP Print (Print-on-demand)
- IngramSpark (40,000+ retailers, libraries)

**Audiobooks:**
- Audible (via ACX or Author's Republic)
- Spotify (80% royalty on direct uploads)
- Apple Books
- Google Play
- Kobo
- Libraries (OverDrive, Hoopla)

## 3. PROMOTION STRATEGY

### Build Your Platform Now (While Still Writing)

Marketing starts before the book is finished. Create a "content ecosystem" around your topic:

**The Three Layers:**
1. **What you know** — Practice being the subject matter expert
2. **People you know** — Your existing network (colleagues, communities)
3. **Where you're known** — Expand into adjacent spaces

### Core Promotion Channels

#### 1. Email List (Your Most Valuable Asset)

Email delivers $36 ROI for every $1 spent. Build your list NOW with:
- Free lead magnets (sample chapter, "5 Principles of Movement Intelligence")
- Behind-the-scenes journey updates
- Exclusive insights not in the book

**Target:** 100-500 engaged subscribers before launch is more valuable than 10,000 casual social media followers.

#### 2. Social Media (Build Trust, Not Just Sales)

**Best platforms for fitness/movement content in 2026:**
- **Instagram** — High visual engagement, Reels for technique/movement clips
- **TikTok** — Short-form video dominates; "FitnessTok" is massive
- **YouTube** — Long-form educational content, demonstrations

**The 80/20 Rule:** 80% value-driven content, 20% promotion

**Content ideas for your book:**
- Before/after transformation clips
- "What I wish I knew" insights
- Movement technique breakdowns
- Real-time training footage
- The emotional/mental side of progress
- Cross-domain carryover demonstrations (surfing, daily life)

#### 3. Podcast Appearances

Podcast audiences are highly engaged and trust host recommendations. This works especially well for nonfiction targeting professional/health-conscious audiences.

**Strategy:**
- Identify 50-100 podcasts in fitness, movement, self-improvement, mindset
- Pitch your unique angle: real-time transformation documentation
- Hire a podcast booking service ($200-400) for 5-10 appearances

#### 4. Amazon Ads (Proven ROI)

Amazon ads remain one of the highest-ROI channels for authors. Case study: A memoir author sold 30k copies in 12 months using only Amazon ads with no social media platform.

**Keywords to target:**
- Competing book titles and authors
- Phrases like "calisthenics journey," "bodyweight strength transformation"
- Movement philosophy terms

#### 5. Pre-Launch Reviews

Start pursuing reviews 8+ weeks before launch:
- NetGalley Pro ($450) for professional reviewers/librarians
- BookSirens for ARC readers
- Goodreads communities
- Fitness book bloggers and Instagram reviewers

### Your Unique Marketing Angles

Based on your book's positioning, these are your differentiators:

1. **Real-time documentation** — You're writing AS you're transforming (rare)
2. **Felt experience focus** — "Here's what it FEELS like" vs. "do this exercise"
3. **Cross-domain carryover** — Surfing, running, daily life improvements
4. **The insight** — "Alignment is organized, not forced" is quotable and memorable
5. **5 concrete skills** — Clear, tangible goals readers can relate to

### Content Strategy for Social Media

**Short-form video ideas (Instagram Reels, TikTok):**
- Reading a paragraph from the book over training footage
- "What changed when I stopped trying harder"
- The breakthrough moment for each skill
- Joint warm-up routines (practical value)
- "What my surfer friends noticed" testimonial-style content

**Longer content (YouTube, blog):**
- Full progression breakdowns
- The science behind "skeleton before muscles"
- Interview-style conversations about the journey
