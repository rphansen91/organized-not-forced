## Key Finding: No Specific Word Count Standards Provided

**Important Note:** Best Seller Publishing's content does NOT focus on specific word counts, page counts, or chapter lengths. Their entire philosophy is **strategy-first, not length-first**. They emphasize:

- The RIGHT readers matter more than the number of pages
- Quality positioning beats quantity of words
- A book should be built around reader transformation, not arbitrary length targets

From their outline article:
> "Try for your outline to be less than 10% of the size of your book."

This is the ONLY specific metric provided across all their content.

## Book Structure Recommendations

### Extracted from "How to Outline a Book" Article

**URL:** https://bestsellerpublishing.org/how-to-outline-a-book/

**The 9-Step Bestseller Framework:**

1. **Define Your Main Outcome**
   - What main outcomes do you want your reader to achieve?
   - What transformation should happen?

2. **Break Into Smaller Outcomes**
   - Each chapter should have its own sub-outcome
   - Chapters should build toward the main transformation

3. **Order Ideas Logically**
   - Plan how each chapter connects to the next
   - Create solid plot progression flow

4. **Start and Close Each Chapter**
   - Every chapter needs a clear start and close
   - Chapter openings should hook; closings should transition

5. **Reorganize as Needed**
   - Don't be discouraged if you need to reorganize
   - Some sections may need stretching; others need condensing

6. **Create Curiosity ("Open Loops")**
   - Tell a story, leave it on a cliffhanger
   - Use cliffhanger as metaphor for teaching point
   - Complete the story at chapter end

7. **Review and Refine**
   - Ensure each section flows smoothly
   - Eliminate redundant information
   - "If it's not impactful, delete it. If it's boring, remove it."

8. **Peer Review**
   - Have others review the outline
   - Fresh eyes catch repetition and unnecessary content

9. **Keep Flexible**
   - Outline should be detailed enough for guidance
   - Flexible enough for creative changes

**Key Quote:**
> "A book outline should be detailed enough to give you a map, but flexible enough to allow you to change directions later on."

## Chapter Structure Recommendations

### From "How to Make Every Chapter Generate Leads" Article

**URL:** https://bestsellerpublishing.org/book-chapter-leads/

**Chapter Framework:**

1. **Start with a Story**
   - Begin chapters with relatable stories
   - Stories create emotional connection

2. **Teach Core Concept**
   - Follow story with teaching/framework
   - Connect story to the lesson

3. **Include Resource Links**
   - Every 2-3 chapters should have a resource/lead magnet
   - NOT just website links—actual valuable content
   - Examples: video series, checklists, blueprints, tip sheets

4. **End with Transition**
   - Close chapters with bridge to next chapter
   - Complete any open loops from the story

**Resource Ideas per Chapter:**
- Short video series going deeper on topic
- Checklist of key steps
- Blueprint to follow
- Downloadable tip sheet
- Course excerpt

**Rob Kosberg's Advice:**
> "Think what are the three, four, five burning questions that my ideal client has... What's keeping them up at night that I can answer for them within chapters of my book?"

## Book Positioning Strategy

### From "Don't Write the Wrong Book" Article

**URL:** https://bestsellerpublishing.org/dont-write-the-wrong-book/

**Three Critical Questions Before Writing:**

1. **Who is the ONE reader?**
   - Not broad categories like "entrepreneurs"
   - Specific: "Burned-out agency owners doing $500K-$2M/year"
   - Your book should SELECT readers, not just attract them

2. **What is the REAL problem?**
   - Go below the surface problem
   - Name the deeper issue better than the reader can
   - Think 2-3 layers below what they initially think

3. **What transformation do they achieve?**
   - What does their life/business look like AFTER reading?
   - Is it better, changed, radically different?

**Key Insight:**
> "Your story can be powerful, but it should not be the center of gravity unless your audience already knows you."

**The Reframe:**
> "The question is not, 'What happened to me?' The better question is, 'How does what happened to me help the reader solve what is happening to them?'"

## Writing Process Recommendations

### From "How to Start Writing a Business Book" Article

**URL:** https://bestsellerpublishing.org/how-to-start-writing-a-business-book/

**Well-Structured Business Book Includes:**
- The framework or philosophy that solves the problem
- Practical steps readers can apply
- Real-world stories or case examples

**Writing Advice:**
- Write 30-60 minutes daily OR complete specific pages weekly
- Small, consistent progress leads to finished manuscript
- Focus on clarity over complexity
- "Readers value clarity more than complexity"
- Build around a SINGLE powerful idea with practical examples

**Common Mistake:**
> "One of the most common mistakes new authors make is trying to include too much information."

### From "Finish Your Book Without Burnout" Article

**URL:** https://bestsellerpublishing.org/finish-your-book-without-burnout/

**Anti-Burnout Framework:**

1. **Define "Done" clearly**
   - Have a specific version of completion
   - Avoid perfectionism paralysis

2. **Measure Progress from Starting Point**
   - Track weekly gains, not just completion
   - Collect proof of movement

3. **Separate Drafting from Judging**
   - Draft sessions: Get ideas out, even rough
   - Revision sessions: Shape with calmer eye
   - Placeholders, imperfect transitions are OK during drafting

4. **Work in Small Weekly Sprints**
   - Create visible gains
   - Momentum comes from structure, not motivation

5. **Name Next Micro Step Before Stopping**
   - Don't end at a clean finish point
   - Write out the next tiny action
   - Makes re-entry easy

## What Makes Books Fail

### From "Why 95% of Books Fail" Article

**URL:** https://bestsellerpublishing.org/why-books-fail/

**Six Failure Patterns:**

1. Idea or audience is unclear
2. No specific goal for the book
3. No real commitment or structure
4. No deadline or timeline
5. No real investment (time, energy, money = no priority)
6. No marketing plan post-publication

**Key Insight:**
> "Books fail because authors tend to write emotionally, not with enough thought... Many authors tend to launch casually, push the publish button, cross their fingers, and then market very little."

### From "Why Most Self-Published Books Fail" Article

**URL:** https://bestsellerpublishing.org/why-most-self-published-books-fail/

**Failure Causes:**
- Trying to appeal to everyone (broad messaging weakens impact)
- Relying solely on Amazon for discovery
- No external traffic sources
- Treating promotion as one-time event
- Writing without defined outcome
- Waiting for perfection (which delays progress)

**Success Factors:**
- Books that speak directly to specific audience with specific problem
- Effective distribution: email lists, social media, podcasts, events, partnerships
- Consistent promotion across multiple channels
- Backend monetization strategy

## Biggest Publishing Mistakes

### From "Biggest Publishing Mistakes First-Time Authors Make" Article

**URL:** https://bestsellerpublishing.org/biggest-publishing-mistakes-first-time-authors/

**Mistake 1: Choosing Too Broad a Topic**
- Specific topics attract committed readers
- Narrow focus can reach wider than broad topics

**Mistake 2: Writing Before Strategy**
- Define audience before writing
- Know what problem you're solving
- Build chapters around reader transformation

**Mistake 3: Weak Amazon/KDP Sales Page**
- Listing should work like a landing page
- Strong cover, clear title/subtitle
- Compelling description
- Reviews, proper categorization

**Mistake 4: Waiting for Traditional Publishing**
- Traditional publishing isn't automatically better
- Self-publishing offers more control
- Don't wait for permission to build something valuable

**Mistake 5: Giving Up After Poor Launch**
- Weak launch ≠ failed book
- Books can be relaunched
- Most readers haven't seen it yet

**Framework for First-Time Authors:**
1. Define exact reader
2. Name main problem book solves
3. Choose narrow topic with business relevance
4. Create title/subtitle built on benefit and clarity
5. Treat Amazon page as conversion asset
6. Plan reviews, traffic, momentum BEFORE release
7. Measure results and revise instead of disappearing

## Memoir + Self-Help Hybrid Recommendations

### Relevant Insights for Your Book

Based on Best Seller Publishing's framework, a calisthenics transformation memoir with 5 skills should:

**Structure:**
1. **Lead with the READER'S problem, not your story**
   - Your transformation becomes proof the method works
   - Story serves the teaching, not the other way around

2. **Each Skill = Chapter or Section**
   - Clear sub-outcomes for each skill
   - Practical steps readers can implement
   - Stories that illustrate the concept

3. **Build Open Loops**
   - Start chapters with transformation moments
   - Use cliffhangers to keep readers engaged
   - Complete loops with teaching points

4. **Include Resources**
   - Every 2-3 chapters, offer deeper content
   - Video tutorials, checklists, progression guides

**Regarding 800-Word Chapters:**

Best Seller Publishing does NOT give specific word counts. However, based on their philosophy:

- If chapters deliver clear transformation and value, length matters less
- If readers are getting stuck or chapters feel incomplete, expand
- If there's "fluff," cut it regardless of length
- Test with beta readers: Do they want more? Or is it complete?

**Their guiding principle:**
> "If it's not impactful, delete it. If it's boring, remove it."

## Bottom Line for Your Book

Best Seller Publishing doesn't provide specific word count targets. Their entire philosophy is:

1. **Strategy before length** - Know your reader, problem, and transformation first
2. **Value over volume** - Delete anything that's not impactful
3. **Reader transformation is the measure** - Does each chapter move them forward?
4. **Test with real readers** - Their feedback matters more than arbitrary standards

**Your 800-word chapters may be:**
- Perfect if they're dense with value
- Too short if concepts feel rushed
- Just right if readers are transformed

The answer isn't on their website—it's in your reader feedback and whether the transformation is happening.
