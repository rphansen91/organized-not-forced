**The pattern across sessions:**

## 💡 SHIFT (~400-600 words)
**The Reframe: Up-Down → Pull-Press**

**Core insight:**
"I think it's really just a mechanics thing I'm not used to the pull press my body is so used to up down"

**What clicked:**
- Brain has thousands of reps of: UP → STOP → DOWN
- Muscle-up requires: UP → FORWARD → PRESS
- Strength was already enough
- Nervous system needed to wire the new pattern

**The cues that worked:**
1. "Pull the bar to my hips" then "Lean over the bar"
2. "Head through the window"
3. "Belt buckle to the bar"
4. Not "pull higher" — "pull → turn → press"

**The mental model:**
Think of muscle-up as: explosive row → immediately dip
Not: high pull-up

**Core Principle (BOLD):**
**"Motor patterns matter more than strength."**
