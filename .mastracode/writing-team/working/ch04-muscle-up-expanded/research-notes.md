- Shoulders give out instantly at transition
- Can't control the descent
- Negatives too advanced
- **Prescribed:** Russian Dips instead, Band-Assisted Transitions

### Session 2: The Height Problem (Message 83)
> "The explosive pulls felt good the form felt good but still didn't have the height to get over the bar I got my chest to the bar but can't get it over"

Coach: "Chest-to-bar with good forward pull = you are past the 'strength' problem. What's missing now is the turnover mechanic, not more height."

**The trap:** "Most people stall here for months because they keep trying to 'pull higher.'"

### Session 3: The Angle Discovery (Message 26-27)
> "I'm instantly feeling more lift keeping my elbows in front of my body but can't quite get enough lift to be able to lean over the bar"

Coach: "You don't need *more height* to lean over the bar. You need a **different angle of pull.**"

Diagram:
- Wrong: ⬆️ Up
- Right: ↗️ Up + Forward

### Session 4: Chin Over, No Press (Message 117)
> "That was it I tried to turn while pulling and I had my chin over and around but couldn't get the chest over to go into the press"

Coach: "YES — that's *exactly* the correct next failure. You reached the hinge point."

Diagnosis: "Your hips stayed slightly too far back. The press never starts if hips don't follow the shoulders."

## 💡 SHIFT — The Reframes

### Reframe 1: Pull Higher → Pull-Turn-Press
Old cue: "Pull higher"
New cue: "Pull → turn → press"

### Reframe 2: Chest Over → Hips Forward
> "You need to think **hips forward**, not chest over."
> "Send hips to the bar."
> "Belt buckle to the bar."

### Reframe 3: Pull-Up Pattern → Row Pattern
> "Think of a muscle-up as: **an explosive row followed immediately by a dip**"
> "ROW → CHEST OVER → DIP"

### Reframe 4: Up-Down → Up-Forward-Press (Message 182-183)
> "Your brain has thousands of reps of: UP → STOP → DOWN"
> "But a muscle-up is: UP → FORWARD → PRESS"
> "Your strength is already enough — your nervous system just hasn't wired the pull → push transition yet."

### Key Cues That Worked:
1. "Pull the bar to my hips" then "Lean over the bar"
2. "Head through the window"
3. "Belt buckle to the bar"
4. "At the top, shove the bar DOWN"
