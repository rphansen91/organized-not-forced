**Opening moment**: First unassisted pistol reps on the left side

**What to include**:

**Reader hook** (within first 5 lines):

**Key sensory details**:

- Multiple reps, not just one lucky attempt

### ⏪ BEAT 2: FLASHBACK (~400 words)

**Where I started**: Wall-assisted pistols, failing by rep 4

**What to include**:
- First session: 5 reps first set, failing on rep 4 both legs for sets 2-3
- The sticking point at the bottom third
- Stability fatigue vs. strength (I had the strength, not the endurance)
- Left quad activating better than right (neural, not strength)

**Key details from source**:
- "failed on the 4th rep both legs for sets 2+3"
- Sticking point "almost certainly at the bottom one-third of the squat"
- "missing repeatability + endurance in the bottom range"

---

### 🔥 BEAT 3: THE STRUGGLE / THE GRIND (~600-800 words)

**This is where we expand significantly**

**Sub-section A: The Hinge Problem**
- Right side "doesn't trust the knee-forward position"
- Body tries to: hinge back, shift load to glute/hip, unload the quad
- The feeling of needing to hinge when hips go below knee
- Left side already trusted the pattern — right side was "almost there"

**Sub-section B: The Bottom Range**
- "hip flexor + deep glute coordination mismatch"
- "losing tension right at the bottom"
- The micro-hesitation: down → pause → up (instead of smooth)
- The cue: "Own the bottom, don't escape it"
- Bottom pause pistols (3×3 each leg, pause 2-3 seconds)

**Sub-section C: Years of Failed Approaches**
- Stretching, yoga, ankle circles, foot massage, calf massage
- The ankle popping that never went away
- Hamstring and hip tightness that always returned
- "If I don't trust this position, I'll keep the muscles tight to protect the joint"

**Key tension**: I had been trying to stretch my way to stability. It wasn't working.

---

### 💡 BEAT 4: THE SHIFT (~400 words)

**The reframe**: Active mobility under load, not passive stretching

**What to include**:
- "Taking the brakes off" — the user's own phrase
- Nervous system protecting joints that don't feel stable
- Stretching affects tissue length temporarily, but doesn't teach safety
- Loading the joint teaches the nervous system: "this range is safe"

**The ankle revelation**:
- Popping in right ankle for years
- Since working on dorsiflexion and pistols, "the popping is starting to ease"
- "still there but it hasn't been this smooth in a while"
- "training single leg squats seems to really be helping"

**Core principle** (bolded):
> **Active mobility under load teaches the nervous system what passive stretching cannot.**

Or: **"Skill work IS the mobility work."**

---

### 🌊 BEAT 5: CARRYOVER (~300 words)

**Surfing proof**: Balance in precarious spots after 2-3 weeks off

**What to include**:
- Hadn't surfed in 2-3 weeks
- 3-4 ft surf, large waves
- "pistols specifically help with my balance in precarious spots"
- The chain: ankle adjusts → knee tracks → hip stabilizes → core counters
- "Your brain now knows how to correct balance before you fall"

**Chapter bridge** (forward momentum):
> The pistol taught me what my ankle needed to learn. But my shoulder was a different problem entirely—one that would take months to untangle.

**Closing**:
> Same ankle. Same knee. Same hip.
> Different relationship between them.
> Organized, not forced.
