## 🔥 THE STRUGGLE (What Wasn't Working)

**The Wall (multiple messages):**
> "sticking point was almost certainly at the bottom one-third of the squat"

**The Problem (Message 142):**
> "Right pistol felt great my left is a bit shaky after my calf cramp but I could do assisted fine"

**Deeper Issue (later messages):**
> "you're losing tension right at the bottom of the pistol"

**What They Were Trying:**
- Passive mobility: stretching, yoga, ankle circles, foot massage, calf massage
- Fighting through the bottom instead of owning it

**The Hinge Problem (Message 120):**
> "I just have a sticking point where I feel like I need to hinge when my hips go below the knee on the right"

**Butt Wink Mechanic:**
> "A butt wink is simply posterior pelvic tilt at the bottom of a squat. In a pistol, it happens when your body runs out of hip flexion range and the pelvis rotates to continue descending."

## 💡 THE SHIFT (What Changed)

**The Insight (Message 189):**
> "Now that I am focusing on the mechanics of these skills it teaches me to use my joints as intended (taking the breaks off)"

**The Ankle Healing (Message 189):**
> "As well as a popping in my right ankle, this is almost even more impressive I've had a popping in my ankle for years but since working on dorsiflexion and pistols the popping is starting to ease still there but it hasn't been this smooth in a while"

**What Failed vs What Worked (Message 189):**
> "I've tried stretching yoga ankle circles foot massage calf massage for years. But training single legs squats seems to really be helping who'd uv thunk it"

**The Reframe:**
- Old: Stretch, massage, passive mobility → hope joint loosens up
- New: Load the joint through movement → nervous system learns control → joint works as intended

**The Cue That Landed:**
> "Own the bottom, don't escape it"

**Why It Worked (from coach analysis):**
> "most long-term tightness comes from the nervous system protecting joints that don't feel stable in certain ranges"
