## 5-Beat Arc Structure

### ⚡ BREAKTHROUGH (300-400 words)
Open with the moment the handstand clicked — when posterior tilt activated and the body became one rigid unit. Fingers controlling balance instead of shoulders chasing it.

### ⏪ FLASHBACK (400-500 words)
Where I was: toe pulls felt controlled but not integrated. Legs wobbling. Wrists screaming after HSPUs. Fighting gravity instead of organizing with it. Shoulder strength chasing balance instead of core creating stability.

### 🔥 STRUGGLE (600-800 words)
The wall that built over weeks:
- Core wasn't activating in inversion — legs floated independently
- Wrist pain from accumulated overhead volume
- HSPUs felt like grinding, not flowing
- Balance corrections at shoulders instead of fingers
- Trying to push "up" harder instead of organizing first

### 💡 SHIFT (400-600 words)
The reframe: before every inverted skill, pre-activate hollow.
- Exhale fully
- Pull ribs down
- Lightly squeeze glutes
- THEN push shoulders tall

"This takes 2 seconds, but it changes everything."

### 🌊 CARRYOVER (300-400 words)
- Surfing pop-up: "scorpion pose" — head up, push down
- Same pattern as planche: protraction + depression + controlled extension
- "stable shoulders + free spine = smooth pop-up"
- The inverted organization applied to everything

## Core Principle

**"Posterior tilt + hollow = everything in calisthenics."**

The inverted organization pattern. Ribs down, glutes on, then shoulders tall. The same principle that unlocks planche, lever, muscle-up, HSPU, and surfing pop-ups.
