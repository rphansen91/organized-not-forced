## Detailed Structure

### ⚡ BREAKTHROUGH (300-400 words)
**Opens with:** The moment of writing down the five goals.

**Sensory details to include:**
- Physical act of writing or typing the list
- The feeling of naming something specific
- The mix of excitement and uncertainty

**Key content:**
1. Muscle-Up
2. Full Planche  
3. Handstand Push-Up
4. Front Lever
5. Pistol Squat

**Reader Problem Hook (within first 5 lines):**
"If you've ever set a goal that felt impossible—if you've ever looked at what others can do and wondered what they know that you don't—this is where my story starts."

---

### ⏪ FLASHBACK (400-500 words)
**Setting:** Where I was before the goals.

**Details to include:**
- 40 pushups, 5 pullups baseline
- The plateau feeling — training without direction
- Watching videos of people with effortless control
- "Strong but not in straight-arm strength territory"
- The gap between what I could do and what I wanted to do

**Tone:** Honest about limitations, not self-pitying. Curious.

---

### 🔥 STRUGGLE (600-800 words)
**The wall:** Why these goals seemed impossible.

**Specific challenges:**
- No straight-arm strength foundation
- Never done any of the five skills
- Didn't know what I didn't know
- The mental gap: "Can someone who starts at 5 pullups actually muscle-up in a year?"
- The uncertainty of the timeline

**Training session details:**
- First planche lean attempt (arms shaking, couldn't hold more than a few seconds)
- First front lever tuck attempt (scaps floating, no depression)
- First pistol attempt (collapsing at the bottom, no control)

**What wasn't working:**
- Grinding through reps
- Watching tutorials
- Collecting cues that didn't stick

---

### 💡 SHIFT (400-600 words)
**The decision:** A different approach.

**Key realizations (foreshadowed, not fully explained):**
- Skills over reps
- Quality over quantity
- Track with video, not just numbers
- Work with a system, not random workouts
- "The goal isn't to get stronger. The goal is to learn what my body needs to learn."

**Core principle planted:**
**"Skills, not reps."** The shift from chasing numbers to chasing capabilities.

---

### 🌊 CARRYOVER (300-400 words)
**What I didn't know yet:**

- The pistol squat would fix ankle mobility that years of stretching couldn't
- The planche would teach scapular organization
- The muscle-up would reveal motor pattern gaps
- Each skill would unlock something beyond itself

**Forward momentum:**
- These weren't just fitness goals
- They were questions my body was asking
- I just didn't know how to listen yet

**Chapter Bridge:**
"I had my goals. I had my timeline. What I didn't have yet was an understanding of how wrong my approach would turn out to be."
