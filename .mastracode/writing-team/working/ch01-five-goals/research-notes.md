# Chapter 1: The Five Goals — Research Notes

## Source Material

### Message 3 (The Original Statement)
> "I want to be able to complete these calisthenics skills by the end of 2026:
> - Muscle Up
> - Full Planche
> - Handstand Push Up
> - Front Lever
> - Pistol Squat"

### Message 4 (Agent Response)
- "40 pushups, 5 pullups, strong base, motivated, building muscle"
- Three concurrent training tracks: Push Mastery, Pull Mastery, Leg Mastery
- Target completion dates ranging from Q2 to Q4 2026

### Message 6 (Timeline Setup)
- 13-month timeline starting Nov 2025
- "You are currently strong (40 pushups, 5 pullups) but not yet in straight-arm strength territory"
- Four phases: Foundation Rebuild → Skill Acquisition → Skill Refinement → Mastery

### From Prologue (For Consistency)
- "Something about it called to me. Not the aesthetics. The *control*."
- "They seemed to understand something about their bodies that I didn't understand about mine."
- "I wasn't weak. I was *disorganized*."

## 5-Beat Arc Structure

### ⚡ BREAKTHROUGH (300-400 words)
Open with the moment of writing down the five goals — the decision to commit. The clarity of having specific targets. The feeling of naming something impossible and deciding to chase it anyway.

### ⏪ FLASHBACK (400-500 words)
Where I was: 40 pushups, 5 pullups. The plateau I'd hit. Training without direction. The sense that something was missing. Watching videos of people who seemed to have unlocked something I couldn't access.

### 🔥 STRUGGLE (600-800 words)
What made these goals seem impossible:
- No straight-arm strength
- Never done a muscle-up
- Never held a planche lean
- No idea what I didn't know
- The gap between "strong enough to do 40 pushups" and "able to hold a planche"
- The uncertainty: was this realistic? Could someone who starts at 5 pullups actually do a muscle-up in a year?

### 💡 SHIFT (400-600 words)
The shift wasn't a technique — it was a commitment to a different approach:
- Skills, not reps
- Organization, not force (foreshadowing the book's thesis)
- Tracking progress with video, not just numbers
- Working with a system, not random workouts

### 🌊 CARRYOVER (300-400 words)
What I didn't know yet: these goals would teach me about my body, not just my strength. The pistol squat would fix my ankle. The planche would teach me scapular organization. Each skill would unlock something beyond itself.
