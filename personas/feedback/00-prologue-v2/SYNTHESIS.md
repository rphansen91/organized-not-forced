# Prologue Feedback Synthesis (v2)

Fresh read from all 5 personas on the original prologue.

---

## Consensus Issues (3+ personas flagged)

### 1. "Something clicked" — 4/5 flagged
**Who:** Marcus, Elena, Derek, Rachel

The phrase is overused transformation language. Everyone says it. It triggers skepticism before you've earned trust.

**But:** The sentences that follow ("My ribs settled...") do the actual work. The issue is the lead-in, not the moment itself.

---

### 2. "Gravity was doing it for me" — 4/5 flagged  
**Who:** Marcus, Elena, Derek, Rachel

Sounds mystical without explanation. Marcus and Derek can infer the mechanism if they work at it, but they shouldn't have to. Elena knows the mechanism but is frustrated you're not stating it.

**The mechanism they want:** In pike position, your torso hangs forward. The weight of your head/ribcage creates a downward pull on the scapulae — passive depression through orientation rather than force.

---

### 3. Surfing feels exclusionary — 4/5 flagged
**Who:** Marcus, Derek, Jordan, Rachel

Marcus: "I don't surf. This feels like a flex."
Derek: "I don't surf. Talk about something I do."  
Jordan: "I can't surf. This feels like content for already-athletic people."
Rachel: "I can barely run. Surfing is aspirational content for healthy people."

**But:** The line "Same ocean. Same board. Different body" is good prose. Elena didn't flag it. The issue isn't the writing — it's the specificity to YOUR life vs. theirs.

---

### 4. Starting point (40 push-ups) alienates beginners — 2/5 flagged
**Who:** Jordan, Rachel

Jordan can do 8 push-ups. Forty sounds elite. Rachel is dealing with chronic limitation — any baseline feels out of reach.

**Note:** This is not consensus (only 2/5). Marcus, Elena, Derek didn't notice.

---

## Non-Consensus Issues (worth noting, not actioning)

### "Effortless" without metrics — 1/5 flagged (Derek only)
Derek wants numbers. "Effortless" isn't a metric. But this is Derek's worldview — the others don't need metrics in a prologue.

### No acknowledgment of pain/limitation — 1/5 flagged (Rachel only)
Rachel wants acknowledgment that bodies resist organizing. Valid concern but prologue may not be the place for this caveat.

### Novel vs. known — 1/5 flagged (Elena only)
Elena wonders if you're reinventing the wheel (PRI, DNS). She's right that you are, but the prologue doesn't need to cite sources.

---

## What's Working

All 5 personas responded positively to:
- **"I wasn't weak. I was disorganized."** — Core reframe landed universally
- **"You can't use muscles to pull your skeleton into place."** — Clear thesis
- **The chain metaphor** — "Ground to torso. Torso to scap..." felt concrete
- **The stuck-ness being real** — Three months on pike push-ups felt authentic

## Decision: What Do We Actually Fix?

Before drafting ANY revisions, we need to answer:

1. **"Something clicked"** — Can we find a phrase that's equally punchy but not cliché? Or do we keep it because the follow-up earns it?

2. **"Gravity was doing it for me"** — Can we add one short phrase that explains the mechanism without dissecting it? The goal is to give just enough that Marcus/Derek stop doing inference work, but not so much that we're writing a PT manual.

3. **Surfing** — Do we cut it? Reframe it? Or keep it and accept that 4/5 personas will skim it?

These are judgment calls. Not formula applications.
