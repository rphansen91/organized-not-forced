# Jordan — The Beginner Dreamer

**Reading:** Prologue (original)  
**Overall Impression:** 7/10 — Inspiring but intimidating

---

## What Lands

**The struggle is real.**
> "I stretched and nothing changed. I ground through reps and stayed stuck."

This is me. I've been trying to get better at push-ups for six months. I do the stretches from Instagram. Nothing changes.

**"This book is for you."**
That line hit. Someone finally said it. Most fitness content feels like it's for people who are already good.

**The five goals.**
Seeing the list — Muscle-Up, Planche, Handstand Push-Up, Front Lever, Pistol Squat — made me excited. That's what I want. Not to be jacked. To be *capable*.

---

## What Triggers My Bullshit Detector

**"I could do forty push-ups. Maybe five pull-ups on a good day."**
This is your "I was just like you" starting point? I can do EIGHT push-ups. ONE pull-up on a good day. You started somewhere I'm trying to GET to. This is humble-bragging.

**Pike position what?**
I had to Google pike position. And scapular depression. This isn't for total beginners. You're writing for people who already have a base.

**The timeline is vague.**
"Six months earlier." "Three months." "A year discovering." But what year of your training was this? Year one? Year five? I can't tell if this took you a year from scratch or a year from already being athletic.

---

## Questions I Still Have

1. **Can someone who starts at 8 push-ups get here?** Or do you need 40 to even begin?
2. **How long does this actually take for a real beginner?**
3. **What are the actual first steps?** The prologue is inspiring but I don't know what to DO.

---

## Where I Almost Put It Down

"I could do forty push-ups." I almost felt excluded right there. Like this is for a more advanced version of me.

---

## What Would Make Me Keep Reading

If you acknowledge that forty push-ups IS a starting point, but the principles work wherever you are. Even one push-up. Just tell me I'm not too far behind.

---

## One-Line Verdict

**I'm inspired but worried this isn't for me yet.**
