# Rachel — The Burnout Cynic

**Reading:** Prologue (original)  
**Overall Impression:** 6.5/10 — Resonates but doesn't acknowledge my reality

---

## What Lands

**"I'd been chasing this feeling for months."**
This is what exhaustion feels like. You chase something and never catch it. You add cues, try harder, and nothing changes. I know this.

**"Finally gotten out of the way."**
As a former dancer, this hits. My best movement was always when I stopped controlling. But that was before my body started fighting back.

**"Alignment isn't forced. It's organized."**
This is what I needed to hear fifteen years ago. Before I pushed through pain. Before I forced my turnout. Before I broke myself trying to make my body do what I saw in mirrors.

---

## What Triggers My Bullshit Detector

**"And then something clicked."**
Things don't click for me. They grind. They occasionally improve. Then they regress. "Clicking" is for young bodies without scar tissue.

**"Effortless. But strong."**
I don't trust effortless. Effortless is a promise that never delivered for me. Everything has cost.

**The whole thing assumes a cooperative body.**
You mention your left shoulder feeling unstable. But you don't mention pain. You don't mention compensation. You don't mention what happens when your body says no. My body says no a lot.

**Surfing.**
Great for you. I can't surf. I can barely run. This feels like content for healthy people doing cool hobbies.

---

## Questions I Still Have

1. **What if gravity doesn't help?** What if hanging forward compresses something that shouldn't be compressed?
2. **Does this work for broken bodies?** Not metaphorically broken. Actually injured. Chronic pain. Structural damage.
3. **What's the cost?** Every system I've tried has a cost I didn't see coming. What's this one?

---

## Where I Almost Put It Down

"Same ocean. Same board. Different body." This is beautiful but it's for someone else. Someone who can paddle out and try things. I'm just trying to sit comfortably.

---

## What Would Make Me Keep Reading

If you acknowledged — even once — that some bodies resist organizing. That this isn't magic. That there are people who might read this and it won't work the same way. Just a sentence that shows you know not everyone's body cooperates.

---

## One-Line Verdict

**The principle resonates. But you're writing from a position of eventual success. I'm reading from a position of chronic limitation.**
