# Marcus — The Frustrated Practitioner

**Reading:** Prologue (original)  
**Overall Impression:** 7.5/10 — I'm interested, but cautiously

---

## What Lands

**The stuck-ness is real.**
> "I'd stacked cue on top of cue, and none of it had worked."

This is my life. Every YouTube video adds another cue. Retract. Depress. Pack. Engage. I have a library of cues and they don't add up to a skill.

**The "effortless but strong" moment.**
That's the thing I can't explain to people. When something finally works, it doesn't feel like MORE effort. It feels like LESS. You captured that.

**"I wasn't weak. I was disorganized."**
This reframes everything. I've been treating this as a strength problem. Maybe it's not.

---

## What Triggers My Bullshit Detector

**"And then something clicked."**
I've heard this exact phrase in fifty YouTube videos. Everyone says something clicked. I need to know WHAT clicked. The next few lines help ("My ribs settled...") but that first line almost lost me.

**"Gravity was doing it for me."**
This sounds mystical. In the pike, gravity is pulling you down — how is that creating scapular depression? I can picture it if I think about it, but you're asking me to do the work you should be doing.

**Surfing.**
Dude, I don't surf. This feels like you're flexing your California lifestyle. The line "Same ocean. Same board. Different body" is beautiful but it's not for me. I skim it.

## Questions I Still Have

1. **How long did this actually take?** You mention "three months" of pike push-ups. But three months from what to what?
2. **What's different about YOUR skeleton?** You mention your left shoulder feeling unstable. Mine too. But you don't explain what was actually wrong.
3. **Is this just pike push-ups?** Or does the principle apply to everything I'm stuck on?
