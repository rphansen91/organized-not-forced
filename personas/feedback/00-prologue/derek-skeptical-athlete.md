# Derek (The Skeptical Athlete) — Prologue Feedback

**Reading as:** 38-year-old former wrestler, CrossFit athlete, 400+ lb deadlift, can kip a muscle-up but not strict, has chronic shoulder issues

---

## Overall Reaction

Alright, I'll give you credit — you didn't start with "here's my perfect muscle-up" humble-brag footage. You started with struggle. That's something.

But I'm watching you closely. If this turns into navel-gazing about "feeling connected" without any actual performance results, I'm out.

---

## What Landed

- **"Trying harder wasn't working."** — Okay, I feel this. My overhead press has been stuck for two years and I've tried everything.

- **"40 push-ups, 5 pull-ups"** — At least you're honest about your starting point. You weren't strong. (I could do 40 push-ups in high school.)

- **"No wasted tension bleeding out through my neck, my biceps, my ribs."** — This actually resonates. When my shoulder is jacked up, I feel like I'm leaking force everywhere. Never had language for it.

- **"Scap to arm. Arm to force. A chain with no leaks."** — This is the kind of thing I want to hear more about. If there's a way to transfer force better, that's useful whether I'm doing bodyweight or barbell.

---

## What Felt Soft

- **Too much poetry, not enough performance.** "Gravity was doing it for me." "Effortless. But strong." Great, did you actually get stronger? Did your numbers go up? Did you hit the five skills? How long did it take?

- **"Something clicked."** — Every Instagram fitness post says this. Show me the before and after. Show me the progression. Show me data.

- **Surfing.** Dude, I don't surf. I don't care about your pop-up. If you want to convince me this transfers to athletics, talk about something universal — running, lifting, throwing. Not your California beach hobby.

---

## Questions I'm Left With

1. **Did you get any stronger in measurable ways?** Not "I felt stronger" — actual numbers. Did your pull-ups go up? Did you add load? Can you do weighted movements now?

2. **How does this apply to my shoulder issues?** You mention your left shoulder felt unstable. Mine does too. Did this fix it? Or is that still a problem?

3. **Would this help my strict muscle-up?** I can kip one all day. Can't get a strict one. Is this relevant to that?

4. **What's your body type?** Are you 155 lbs and lean? Because bodyweight skills are a different game for someone who's 195 and built for wrestling.

---

## Where I Almost Put It Down

**"I wasn't weak. I was disorganized."** — Look, I don't know you. You might have been weak AND disorganized. If the whole book is going to be about how strength doesn't matter, I'm skeptical. Strength matters. Force production matters. Organization without force is just posture.

---

## What Would Keep Me Reading

- Performance outcomes (did the skills get achieved? when? how long?)
- Application to real athletics beyond calisthenics
- Something about shoulder health that doesn't require me to give up lifting
- Acknowledgment that strength still matters

---

## Honest Rating

**6/10** — Promising start, but I'm not sold yet. The "organized not forced" idea is interesting, but it sounds like it could easily become an excuse for weakness. Prove me wrong.

---

## One Line Summary

*"I'm interested in the force transfer stuff, but if this is just a philosophy book dressed up as training advice, I'm not finishing it."*
