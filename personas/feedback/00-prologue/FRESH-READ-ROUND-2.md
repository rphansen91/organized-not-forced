## Elena (The Movement Nerd)

**Score: 8/10** (was 7.5/10)

### First Impressions

The "inverted, gravity was on my side" line is actually smart biomechanically. In pike, you're creating a closed chain where the scapula can find its position without the upper traps fighting gravity. He's discovered scapulohumeral rhythm through feel rather than theory—which is interesting.

### What Landed

- "You can't use muscles to pull your skeleton into place." — This is essentially motor control theory in plain language. The body organizes around constraints, not commands.
- "Alignment isn't forced. It's *organized*." — Good framing. It's not novel to the PT world, but the way he's arrived at it experientially is valuable.
- "I've always been an athlete" — he's not starting from dysfunction, he's starting from competence. That's actually refreshing. Most movement books are about fixing broken people.

### Remaining Concerns

- I hope he acknowledges existing frameworks at some point. This isn't NEW, but his experiential pathway to it could be.
- The "skeleton before muscles" idea needs more development. What does that actually mean in practice?

### Would I Keep Reading?

Yes. Curious if he maintains intellectual honesty as he gets into specific skills.
